Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of flat pasta, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. The pasta sheets are traditionally boiled before layering, though no-boil varieties are also popular. Each layer is generously covered with sauce and cheese, creating a hearty and comforting meal. It is baked until bubbling and golden, with a crispy top layer. Lasagna is often served as a main course, accompanied by a side salad or garlic bread.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles-Preferably pre-cooked or no-boil to simplify preparation.
  • Tomato sauce-A simple marinara or your preferred tomato-based sauce.
  • Vegetables-For the fillingo; a mix of vegetables.
  • Cheese-Mozzarella and ricotta are commonly used, but one can be sufficient.
  • Seasonings-Basic spices like salt, pepper, and Italian herbs (e.g., oregano, basil) to enhance the flavor.

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients:Cook the ground meat or vegetables if using, and mix with tomato sauce. Pre-cook lasagna noodles if not using no-boil.
  2. Layer the ingredients:Spread a thin layer of sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, followed by sauce, and cheese. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of cheese on top.
  3. Bake:Cover the dish with foil and bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  4. Cool slightly:Let the lasagna rest for about 10 minutes before slicing to help it set.
  5. Serve:Cut into pieces and serve hot.
